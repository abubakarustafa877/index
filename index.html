Cricket Career

  • He began his international cricket career at age 18, in a 1971 Test series against England.

    • Test Career

    He ended his career with 88 Test matches, 126 innings and scored 3807 runs at an average of 37.69,
    including six centuries and 18 fifties. His highest score was 136. As a bowler, he took 362 wickets
    in Test cricket, which made him the first Pakistani and world's fourth bowler to do so.

    ODI Career

    In ODIs, he played 175 matches and scored 3709 runs at an average of 33.41. His highest score
    was 102 not out. His best ODI bowling was 6 wickets for 14 runs, a record for the best bowling
    figures by any bowler in an ODI innings in a losing cause.

    Captaincy

    In 1982 Imran Khan took over the captaincy of the Pakistan cricket team from Javed Miandad.
